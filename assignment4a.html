Experimental Method · 2 of 2

Test Procedure

Same script followed with every participant.

1
Brief introduction PETTEL is like Airbnb — for finding people to care for your pet while you're away. No further instructions given.
2
Task 1 — Create a pet profile Participant told what to achieve, not how. Encouraged to think out loud.
3
Task 2 — Find & book a sitter Given after Task 1 is complete.
4
Task 3 — Track your pet Given after Task 2. Participant navigates freely.
Hint rule: If stuck for over 2 minutes, facilitator points to one button only. Participant continues independently after that.

Improvement Suggestions

What we're changing

Issues observed across all 3 sessions, grouped by priority.

📱
High priority
UI click targets too small
Increase button and icon sizes with larger padding across all desktop screens. Flagged by all 3 participants.
📋
High priority
No booking history after confirmation
Add a "My Bookings" screen showing status, sitter info and dates. Flagged by 2 of 3 participants.
🗺️
Medium priority
Map is static, price markers unclear
Enable interactive pan and zoom. Redesign price chips for legibility on the map view.
✨
Medium priority
Smart Match lacks depth
Add granular filter tags: allergy type, specific diet restrictions, stress tolerance level.
Priority rationale: Mobile usability issues affect all 3 users — highest priority. Booking history is a direct trust signal tied to PETTEL's core value of Transparency.
